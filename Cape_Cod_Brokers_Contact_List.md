## 💡 Tips for Successful Outreach

### **Before Contacting:**
1. **Prepare your elevator pitch:** "I'm launching a tech-enabled property management business for Cape Cod vacation rentals and need broker supervision."
2. **Have documents ready:** Business plan summary, service menu, your background
3. **Know your ask:** Clear about supervision needs, flexible on details
4. **Research each broker:** Mention something specific about their business

### **During Conversations:**
1. **Focus on mutual benefit:** How does this help THEIR business?
2. **Emphasize your differentiation:** Tech expertise is rare in property management
3. **Be transparent:** About your experience level, goals, timeline
4. **Listen more than talk:** Understand their concerns and needs

### **Negotiation Points:**
- **Commission split:** Start asking for 70/30 (you get 70%), settle for 60/40
- **Autonomy:** You handle day-to-day, they handle compliance
- **Term:** 6-month trial period, then month-to-month
- **Exclusivity:** Typically non-exclusive (you can work with other brokers)
- **Insurance:** Clarify who carries what insurance

## ⚠️ Red Flags to Watch For

### **Avoid brokers who:**
- Want more than 50% commission for minimal involvement
- Have poor reputation in Cape Cod community
- Don't understand property management regulations
- Aren't responsive during initial conversations
- Want excessive control over your business operations
- Have unclear insurance/liability arrangements

### **Warning signs:**
- Unwilling to put agreement in writing
- Vague about their responsibilities
- History of complaints with MA Board
- Too many restrictions on your operations
- Unreasonable fees beyond commission split
