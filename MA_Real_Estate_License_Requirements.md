# Massachusetts Real Estate Broker License Requirements for Property Management
**Researched:** March 19, 2026  
**For:** Cape Cod Property Management Business

---

## 🎯 Quick Summary

**Yes, you need a real estate broker license to manage properties for others in Massachusetts.** This includes vacation rental management where you handle leasing, rent collection, or property operations for compensation.

---

## 📋 License Requirements Overview

### **1. Education Requirements**
- **40 hours** of approved pre-licensing education
- Must be completed at an approved real estate school
- Courses cover: MA real estate law, ethics, contracts, property management
- **Cost:** $300-$600 for courses

### **2. Experience Requirements** (Choose ONE path)
#### **Path A: Salesperson Experience**
- Hold active MA real estate salesperson license for **1 year**
- Work under a licensed broker during that year
- Complete required transactions (varies by board)

#### **Path B: Direct Experience**
- **3 years** of full-time real estate experience
- Experience must be under supervision of licensed broker
- Can include property management experience
- Must be verifiable with documentation

### **3. Examination**
- Pass the **Massachusetts Real Estate Broker Exam**
- Computer-based test at approved testing centers
- Covers: Real estate principles, practices, MA-specific laws
- **Exam Fee:** ~$75-100
- **Passing Score:** Typically 70-75%

### **4. Application Process**
1. Complete education requirements
2. Gain required experience
3. Pass broker exam
4. Submit application to MA Board of Registration of Real Estate Brokers and Salespersons
5. **Application Fee:** $150-200
6. Background check with fingerprinting
7. Wait for approval (4-8 weeks typical)

### **5. Ongoing Requirements**
- **License Renewal:** Every 2 years
- **Renewal Fee:** ~$100-150
- **Continuing Education:** 12 hours every 2 years
- Must maintain errors & omissions insurance
- Must affiliate with a brokerage (or establish your own)

## 🏢 Alternative Paths & Strategies

### **Option 1: Work Under a Licensed Broker** (Recommended for Startup)
- **How it works:** You work as an employee/independent contractor under an existing broker
- **Requirements:** Only need salesperson license (easier to obtain)
- **Pros:**
  - Faster startup (no broker license needed initially)
  - Learn from experienced broker
  - Lower initial costs
  - Broker handles legal compliance
- **Cons:**
  - Split commissions with broker
  - Less control over business
  - Still need salesperson license

### **Option 2: Salesperson License First**
- **Steps:**
  1. Get salesperson license (24 hours education, no experience required)
  2. Work under broker for 1 year
  3. Then apply for broker license
- **Salesperson License Requirements:**
  - 24 hours pre-licensing education
  - Pass salesperson exam
  - Background check
  - Application fee: ~$100

### **Option 3: Partnership Model**
- Partner with someone who already has broker license
- They handle legal/compliance side
- You handle operations/marketing
- Split ownership accordingly

### **Option 4: Limited Services Model** (Riskier)
- Only offer services that don't require license:
  - Cleaning coordination (no rent collection)
  - Maintenance coordination (no leasing decisions)
  - Marketing services only (no tenant screening)
  - Tech installation services (separate business)
- **Warning:** Gray area - consult attorney before pursuing

## 🏝️ Cape Cod Specific Considerations

### **Local Regulations**
- **Short-term Rental Registration:** Required with MA state ($50 fee)
- **Local Taxes:** Cape Cod towns collect occupancy taxes (4-6%)
- **Zoning:** Verify property is zoned for short-term rentals
- **Health/Safety:** Must meet state sanitary code requirements
- **Insurance:** Special vacation rental insurance needed

### **Practical Advice for Cape Cod**
1. **Network with local brokers** - Many may be open to supervision arrangement
2. **Join Cape Cod & Islands Association of Realtors** - Networking and resources
3. **Understand seasonal patterns** - License process may align with off-season
4. **Consider dual licensing** - If managing properties in other states

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### **Q: Can I manage my own properties without a license?**
**A:** Yes, managing your own properties doesn't require a license.

### **Q: What if I only handle maintenance/cleaning, not rent?**
**A:** Possibly exempt, but consult attorney. "Property management" broadly defined in MA.

### **Q: How strict is enforcement on Cape Cod?**
**A:** Increasingly strict as short-term rental market grows. Not worth the risk.

### **Q: Can I get licensed in another state more easily?**
**A:** Some states have easier requirements, but you need MA license for MA properties.

### **Q: What about smart home installation - need separate license?**
**A:** Possibly - electrical work may require license. Low-voltage (data/AV) often doesn't.

### **Q: How much can I make while working under a broker?**
**A:** Typical splits: 50/50 to 70/30 (you get larger share). $100k+ possible with 10+ properties.
